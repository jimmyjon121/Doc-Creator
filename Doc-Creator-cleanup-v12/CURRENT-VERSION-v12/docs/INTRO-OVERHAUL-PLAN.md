# CareConnect Pro Intro Overhaul
## "A Day in the Life of a Clinical Coach"

### Target Audience
Clinical coaches at Family First Adolescent Services who:
- Manage 8-15 clients simultaneously
- Must track regulatory compliance deadlines (Day 14, Day 30, etc.)
- Coordinate with families on aftercare planning
- Need to find appropriate programs for each client's unique needs
- Create documentation for discharge packets
- Feel overwhelmed by the complexity of their role

### Emotional Arc
1. **Recognition** - "This is exactly what my day looks like"
2. **Relief** - "Finally, something designed for how I actually work"
3. **Confidence** - "I can see everything I need in one place"
4. **Empowerment** - "I can actually get ahead instead of just keeping up"

---

## Scene-by-Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1: The Clinical Reality (20 seconds)
**Purpose**: Create immediate identification - "This is MY life"

**Visual Elements**:
- Animated desk scene (keep hand-drawn warmth)
- But show CLINICAL chaos, not generic chaos:
  - Kipu EMR tab open
  - Family voicemail notifications
  - "Day 14 review due tomorrow" sticky note
  - Discharge planning meeting reminder
  - Stack of aftercare program brochures
  - Calendar with overlapping appointments

**Voiceover Script**:
> "You've got Kipu open in one tab, a family calling about aftercare options, and three clients hitting Day 14 this week. Discharge planning for Marcus starts tomorrow, but you still haven't found the right IOP for Sarah's insurance."
>
> *(pause as chaos intensifies)*
>
> "Sound familiar?"
>
> "There's a better way."

**Key Difference**: Use REAL clinical terminology and scenarios, not generic "busy professional" imagery.

## Visual Style Guidelines

### Color Palette (Clinical, Not Cyberpunk)
- **Background**: Deep navy (#0F172A) - professional, calming
- **Primary**: Clinical teal (#0D9488) - healthcare, trust
- **Secondary**: Calm cyan (#0891B2) - clarity, focus
- **Accents**: 
  - Sage green (#10B981) - growth, wellness
  - Warm amber (#FBBF24) - attention, warmth
- **Alerts**: Clinical red (#DC2626) - urgency without panic

### Animation Philosophy
- **Purposeful**: Every animation should demonstrate functionality
- **Calm**: No jarring transitions, smooth easing
- **Clinical**: Professional, not playful
- **Efficient**: Show speed and ease, not complexity

### Typography
- Clean, readable sans-serif
- Generous spacing
- Clear hierarchy
- No tech jargon in UI labels

### Voiceover Tone
- Warm but professional
- Empathetic ("Sound familiar?")
- Confident but not salesy
- Clinical vocabulary where appropriate
